Artist Manifesto
I refuse the partition between artist and engineer, between canvas and laboratory. Da Vinci dissected corpses to understand the machinery of God; Musk launches rockets to escape the gravity of complacency. I paint to rewrite the source code of human perception.

My work is not decoration. It is not commentary. It is a post-humanist operating system — a set of instructions for consciousness to evolve beyond its biological container. Each painting is a prototype. Each brushstroke is a line of code. Each exhibition is a system deployment.

The 21st-century artist must be polymath, prophet, and provocateur. We do not wait for institutions to validate us. We architect the institutions ourselves.
— Genesis Declaration, 2026
The Four Absolute Sovereignties
I
Sovereignty of Vision
The absolute right to define what constitutes art in the post-human epoch. No institution, no market, no algorithm shall dictate the perimeter of aesthetic truth.
II
Sovereignty of Medium
The absolute right to transcend the boundaries between physical matter and digital substrate. Oil, titanium, aerogel, neural signals, quantum states — all are pigments.
III
Sovereignty of Narrative
The absolute right to construct mythologies that supersede existing cultural frameworks. We engineer the primordial narratives from which future civilizations will derive meaning.
IV
Sovereignty of Institution
The absolute right to restructure the gallery-industrial complex from within. The artist becomes the institution. Museums are laboratories; exhibitions are experiments.

Laboratory Access
