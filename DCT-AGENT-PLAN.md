### 邮件 ①：Noname Studio 上海

**To:** noname.studio@yahoo.com
**Subject:** Artist Residency Application — Mengzhen Yang (DCT)

Dear Noname Studio Selection Committee,

I am writing to formally apply for the artist residency program at Noname Studio in Zhujiajiao, Shanghai.

Mengzhen Yang (DCT) is a Chinese-American visual artist born in 1993 in Ma'anshan, China, with nearly a decade of professional practice between South Korea and the United States.

Her work has been exhibited internationally, including the Boston Asia Contemporary Art Exhibition (BACAF 2016), the World Trade Center's THE OCULUS ART SHOW in New York, and the Council of Asian Designers of America (CADA) exhibition at 415 Broadway, New York. She has shown with CHAMELEON GALLERY in New York and collaborated with LA-based street fashion brand 212'FUN.

In 2020, Yang was recognized as one of the AACYF Top 30 Under 30 Outstanding Chinese in the United States and received a Certificate of Congressional Recognition from U.S. Representative Gilbert R. Cisneros Jr. (California, 39th District). She served as Artist Coordinator of the California Music and Art Alliance (CAMAC).

Yang holds a recommendation from Prof. Xiang Jian, Lecturer at the University of Southern California (USC), Department of East Asian Languages and Cultures, who is also a collector of her oil paintings.

For over a decade, Yang has worked exclusively by hand — oil on canvas, charcoal, watercolor — creating large-scale, physically intensive paintings built through palette knife and impasto techniques. Her painting "I Am a Growth" took over three years to complete. Most of her works originate from dreams. Recently, she has begun exploring the intersection of traditional hand-painting and digital media.

RESIDENCY PROPOSAL
I wish to apply for a 2–3 month residency to develop a new series of large-scale oil paintings exploring the convergence of Eastern landscape tradition and contemporary abstraction. The 300sqm studio would be ideal for my practice. I am pleased to donate one work to the foundation collection.

PORTFOLIO: https://realid-zz.github.io/pencil/
REFERENCE: Prof. Xiang Jian, USC — xjian@usc.edu

Respectfully,
Mengzhen Yang (DCT)
hihelloaikenni@gmail.com
