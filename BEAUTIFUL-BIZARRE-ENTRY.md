## Artist Biography（复制下面这段，1490字符）

```
Mengzhen Yang (b. 1993, Ma'anshan, Anhui, China) is a visual artist based in Los Angeles. Working primarily in oil painting, she builds each work over months and years — her painting "I Am a Growth" took over three years of continuous creation. Yang's practice spans physical painting, digital illustration, and generative art, unified by an uncompromising aesthetic she calls "Death Pencil" — raw, confrontational, and rooted in the tension between control and chaos.

Her oil paintings employ aggressive impasto and palette-knife techniques, channeling expressive force through the sheer physicality of paint. Her digital works extend this intensity into algorithmic territory — generative brushstrokes and glitch overlays that interrogate the boundary between hand and machine.

Yang's work explores sovereignty, transformation, and the collapse of identity into archetypal form. She refuses the partition between artist and engineer, treating every brushstroke — whether made by knife or by code — as an act of reconstruction. Her portfolio includes large-scale oil paintings, a portrait glitch series merging charcoal drawing with digital disruption, and procedural generative artworks.

Portfolio: https://realid-zz.github.io/pencil/
```

**Artwork Statement（复制，790字符）：**

```
I Am a Growth is an oil painting created over more than three years — a durational work born from sustained physical engagement with paint and canvas. The composition is built through aggressive impasto technique: thick, sculptural layers of green, orange, purple, and blue collide and overlap, each passage recording a different moment in a years-long conversation between artist and material.

Green dominates as the color of life and persistence. The dark purples and blacks push inward like gravitational forces, while bursts of orange and pink erupt outward. The painting captures a jungle world — dense, layered, teeming with implied narrative — yet the overall presence remains demure and calm. It is a portrait of growth itself: not gentle or decorative, but brave, raw, and relentlessly alive.
```
