**Personal Motivation / Why Swatch（复制）：**

```
I am an oil painter who works slowly and physically. Each painting takes months or years — "I Am a Growth" took three years. The Swatch Art Peace Hotel residency would give me the sustained time and space I need to develop a new body of large-scale works in Shanghai, a city whose energy and contradictions directly feed my practice.

I want to create a new series exploring "floating human figures" — bodies suspended between materiality and dissolution, painted through aggressive impasto and palette-knife technique. Shanghai's layered history — the Bund's colonial architecture, the density of the city, the tension between old and new — mirrors the tension in my paintings between control and chaos, figuration and abstraction.

The 3-6 month duration is ideal for my process. I do not make quick paintings. I build them.
```

**What Makes You Unique（复制）：**

```
My practice is defined by extreme physicality and duration. I paint with palette knives, not brushes. I build surfaces that are sculptural — thick, layered, aggressive. My painting "I Am a Growth" took over three years and was priced at $60,000. I also maintain a Portrait Glitch series where hand-drawn charcoal portraits are disrupted by digital overlays, and I write generative code that translates painterly gesture into algorithm. But the center is always the canvas, the body, the knife, the hours.
```

**Planned Work in Shanghai（复制）：**

```
A new series of 4-6 monumental oil paintings (120-180 cm each) exploring floating human figures through impasto technique. The series investigates the moment when a recognizable body dissolves into pure material — color, texture, gravity, drip. I will also continue my Portrait Glitch series using Shanghai street photography as source material, creating a dialogue between the city and the disrupted portrait.
```

```
Mengzhen Yang (b. 1993, Ma'anshan, Anhui, China) is a visual artist working primarily in oil painting. Her practice also spans charcoal drawing, watercolor, digital illustration, and generative art — unified by an uncompromising aesthetic she calls "Death Pencil." **Artwork Description（Crimson Sovereign，复制）：**
```

```
Crimson Sovereign is a monumental oil painting rendered through aggressive palette-knife impasto. The fox emerges from a field of violent color — deep crimsons, yellows, and greens collide in a composition that channels the raw energy of the animal into the physical materiality of the paint. Each stroke carries the full weight of the artist's body. The work refuses the decorative; it demands confrontation.
```

**Artwork Description（Jungle Descent，复制）：**

```
Jungle Descent is an immersive oil painting built through dense layering of deep blues, greens, and blacks. Vines cascade downward, flowers bloom in darkness, and ladders emerge from the undergrowth — a world simultaneously growing and falling. White drip elements create vertical gravity, pulling the eye through layers of botanical chaos into an abyss of color and form.
```
